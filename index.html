Group Urging Ban on Nazi Memorabilia Sales Asks Court to Dismiss Yahoo! Lawsuit

2/8/01 La Ligue Contre Le Racisme Et L'Antisemitisme Fought For Removal of Hate
Materials From Yahoo! Commercial Sites.

SANTA CLARA, Calif., Feb. 8 -- The French-based organization La Ligue Contre Le Racisme Et L'Antisemitisme (LICRA), which fought to ban the sale of Nazi memorabilia on the Yahoo! (Nasdaq: YHOO) commercial sites, has filed a motion with the U.S. District Court in San Jose to dismiss Yahoo!'s lawsuit against the human rights volunteer group.

In December, 2000, Yahoo! asked a California Court in San Jose to overrule a French court's decision ordering Yahoo! to block the sale of Nazi memorabilia or face the possibility of a daily fine of 100,000 French francs if it did not establish methods to block the sale of such hate-related items.

Yahoo!, one of the most popular Web Site portals for searching the Internet, was embroiled in controversy over permitting the sale of hate items, such as Nazi memorabilia and Ku Klux Klan paraphernalia on its auction site. The company has since announced modifications to monitor and restrict the sale of such material. According to the company's statement, "Yahoo! will no longer allow items that are associated with groups which promote or glorify hatred and violence to be listed on any of Yahoo!'s commerce properties." A monitoring system was also launched to enforce the refined policy and to identify items that do not comply with the practice.

Nevertheless, Yahoo!'s suit against LICRA has not been withdrawn by the company. Indeed, Yahoo! has recently sought a default judgement against LICRA.

At that point, LICRA retained Ronald S. Katz, of the international law firm, Coudert Brothers, which has offices in both San Jose and Paris. Mr. Katz, a partner representing LICRA, said, "I do not understand why Yahoo! is persisting in this lawsuit after it has supposedly changed its policy regarding Nazi memorabilia. It makes me wonder whether their change in policy is sincere. LICRA has absolutely no contacts with the United Sates. It is a voluntary organization in France that opposes racism and anti-Semitism. I think that there is virtually no chance that the court will find that Yahoo! has jurisdiction over LICRA in San Jose, California."
